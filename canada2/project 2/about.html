About Cryptocurrencies

Security: Cryptocurrencies use cryptographic techniques to secure transactions and control the creation of new units. This security feature makes it challenging for hackers to manipulate the system or counterfeit digital currency.

Anonymity and Transparency: While cryptocurrencies offer a level of privacy through the use of pseudonymous addresses, all transactions are publicly recorded on the blockchain, providing transparency and traceability.

Limited supply: Most cryptocurrencies have a finite supply, which means there is a maximum number of coins or tokens that can ever be created. This scarcity can contribute to their value over time.

Bitcoin (BTC): Created in 2009, Bitcoin was the first and remains the most well-known cryptocurrency. It serves as a store of value and a medium of exchange

Ethereum (ETH): Launched in 2015, Ethereum is more than just a cryptocurrency; it is a decentralized platform that enables the creation of smart contracts and decentralized applications (DApps).
